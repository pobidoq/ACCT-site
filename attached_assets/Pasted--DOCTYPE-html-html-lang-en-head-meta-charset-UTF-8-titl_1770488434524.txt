      <section id="problem">
        <h2>The problem: documentation without durable accountability</h2>
        <p>
          High‑stakes investigations, regulatory actions, and inquiries generate millions of pages
          of public documentation. Yet only a fraction is ever analyzed in depth, every newsroom or
          NGO rebuilds timelines from scratch, and there is no shared, structured record of what
          has been questioned, clarified, or resolved.
        </p>
        <div class="section-grid" style="margin-top: 12px;">
          <div class="card">
            <h3>Today’s reality</h3>
            <ul>
              <li>Document dumps dominate headlines, then disappear into PDFs and archives.</li>
              <li>No common backbone for journalists, researchers, and advocacy groups.</li>
              <li>Public cannot easily see progress from “exposed” to “addressed”.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
          <div class="card">
            <h3>What is missing</h3>
            <ul>
              <li>A shared map of unresolved vs. clarified accountability questions.</li>
              <li>Incentives and infrastructure for careful long‑term review.</li>
              <li>A transparent signal layer that others can plug into and trust.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </div>
      </section>

      <section id="how-it-works">
        <h2>How pobidoq works</h2>
        <p>
          pobidoq turns static documentation into structured accountability units, reviews them
          through a Human–AI loop, and records evolving accountability states as on‑chain indices.
        </p>
        <div class="section-grid">
          <div class="card">
            <h3>1. Ingest & structure</h3>
            <p>
              Public or partner‑provided documents are imported, cleaned, and broken into stable,
              referenceable segments. Each segment is linked back to its source and versioned for
              integrity.
            </p>
            <div class="pill-row">
              <span class="pill">Document ingestion</span>
              <span class="pill">OCR & parsing</span>
              <span class="pill">Segment IDs & hashes</span>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="card">
            <h3>2. Surface accountability questions</h3>
            <p>
              Software suggests potential issues—contradictions, timeline gaps, process failures,
              recurring entities. These are prompts for human review, not verdicts.
            </p>
            <div class="pill-row">
              <span class="pill">Anomaly detection</span>
              <span class="pill">Pattern mining</span>
              <span class="pill">Issue candidates</span>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="card">
            <h3>3. Human–AI Review (HAR)</h3>
            <p>
              Journalists, researchers, advocacy groups, and vetted community members confirm,
              refine, or dispute these questions. Their work is saved as structured review
              artifacts.
            </p>
            <div class="pill-row">
              <span class="pill">Contradiction workflows</span>
              <span class="pill">Process‑failure mapping</span>
              <span class="pill">Network exposure</span>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="card">
            <h3>4. Accountability indices</h3>
            <p>
              Validated findings update accountability units and aggregated indices. These indices
              are mirrored on‑chain so that rules and changes remain transparent and auditable.
            </p>
            <div class="pill-row">
              <span class="pill">On‑chain state</span>
              <span class="pill">Auditability</span>
              <span class="pill">Composability</span>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </section>
